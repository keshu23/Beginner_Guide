Language Levels

Explore different levels of programming languages and their characteristics

Low Level

Machine-dependent (0s and 1s) language.

Runs directly without translation, offering peak performance.

Examples: Machine code, Assembly.

High Level

User-friendly, designed for rapid development.

Requires a compiler or interpreter for execution.

Easy to read, write, and maintain across platforms.

Examples: Python, Java, JavaScript, C#.

Middle Level

Bridges the gap between hardware and abstraction.

Supports low-level memory access with high-level syntax.

Examples: C, C++.
